1899 - The Beginning

Matchless was founded in 1899 by Henry Collier in Plumstead, London. Initially, the company produced bicycles, but soon transitioned to motorcycles, marking the start of an iconic journey.

1907 - The First TT Win

In 1907, Charlie Collier, son of the founder, won the inaugural Isle of Man TT race on a Matchless motorcycle, cementing the brand's reputation in the world of competitive racing.

1930s - Expansion and Innovation

The 1930s saw Matchless expand its range, introducing new models that featured advanced engineering and design, including the famous G3 model.

1941 - World War II Contributions

During World War II, Matchless produced thousands of motorcycles for the military, including the G3L, which became one of the most widely used bikes by the Allied forces.

1960s - The AMC Era

In the 1960s, Matchless became part of the Associated Motorcycles (AMC) group, continuing to innovate and produce popular models until the eventual decline of the British motorcycle industry.

Today - A Legacy Lives On

Though production ceased in the late 1960s, the legacy of Matchless lives on. Enthusiasts and collectors around the world continue to cherish and restore these classic motorcycles.
